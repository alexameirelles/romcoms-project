Are All Rom-Com Women
Journalists?
You may get this impression, I know I did.
Alexa Meirelles | June 28, 2026

Spoiler alert: no, obviously not every female lead in a romantic comedy is a journalist. But there is a reason so many people get that impression. If you're a fan of the genre like I am — a 29-year-old journalist who grew up watching these movies, especially as a teenager — you probably remember characters such as Jenna Rink from 13 Going on 30, Andie Anderson from How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days, Sally Albright from When Harry Met Sally, or even the 2000s most popular anti-heroine Carrie Bradshaw from Sex and the City, whose success began on television before making its way to the big screen.

Some were newspaper reporters, others worked for magazines, while some were columnists, critics, or television news producers. Regardless of the exact role, the archetype became deeply ingrained in our collective imagination: the fearless, intelligent, charismatic, workaholic journalist. Sometimes she was at the peak of her career; other times she was barely keeping it together, but she was almost always chasing the next big story.

Of course, Hollywood's journalism versions were far from realistic. In a 2023 Vogue article "How Did Journalism Become the It Career for Rom-Com Protagonists?", reporter Emma Specter revisits one of the most iconic scenes in 13 Going on 30, in which Jenna Rink proposes a complete rebrand for Poise magazine and almost instantly wins everyone over. Could anything be less realistic?

It's worth noting that these characters existed during a very different era for the profession. Journalism has never exactly been synonymous with money and power for most people who work in it, but for a time it was associated with a certain glamorous lifestyle, at least on screen.

But that's part of the charm of a good romantic comedy: it doesn't have to reflect reality faithfully. To find out how common the profession really is, I analyzed the IMDb movies database, focusing on romantic comedies released between 1980 and 2025 that have received at least 10,000 ratings on IMDb.

The definition of a romantic comedy can be a bit fuzzy. Some films lean more toward drama, while in others romance isn't really the central focus of the story. To keep the dataset consistent, I kept only movies that feature at least one central couple whose relationship is a driving force of the narrative, and that are popularly regarded as rom-coms.

That left me with 882 movies, not just Hollywood productions, although they make up the overwhelming majority. So, what do the numbers actually show?

The genre grew rapidly in the 1990s and peaked in the 2000s, the decade that gave us some of its most iconic titles, including 13 Going on 30, How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days, The Proposal, 27 Dresses, Bridget Jones's Diary and others.

And here's why wondering "are they all journalists?" isn't too absurd. It is indeed one of the ten most common professions among female romantic comedy protagonists. Even so, stories about teenagers and young adults dominate the genre: "student" appears far more often than any other occupation.

Among male protagonists, the picture changes. Businessman is the most common profession, reflecting one of the genre's most familiar archetypes: the successful, powerful man who initially clashes with the female lead before eventually becoming her love interest. Writer comes in second, making it another remarkably common occupation among rom-com heroes.

Methodology. This analysis is based on three publicly available IMDb datasets: title.basics, which contains core information about each film, including genres and release year; title.ratings, used to filter for movies with at least 10,000 user ratings; and title.principals, which provides information about the main cast and crew. I selected films released between 1980 and 2025 that were tagged with both Comedy and Romance. Since IMDb genre tags alone do not necessarily correspond to what critics and audiences would recognize as romantic comedies, I conducted a second round of manual review in batches to remove films that, despite carrying both genre labels, were not primarily considered rom-coms. To identify the professions of protagonists, I used the OpenAI API to classify characters based on contextual information. The resulting classifications were then manually reviewed in batches, with missing values filled in and automated labels checked for consistency and accuracy.
